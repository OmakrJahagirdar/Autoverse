AutoVerse Industries was founded in 1989 by Rajan Mehta with a single CNC machine and a bold vision: to create a world-class automotive components company rooted in Indian engineering talent and global manufacturing standards.

From those humble beginnings in a 2,000 sq. ft. workshop in Pimpri-Chinchwad, AutoVerse has grown into a 280,000 sq. ft. multi-plant operation with over 2,000 employees and a presence in 50+ countries.

Company History

+

AutoVerse Industries Pvt. Ltd. was incorporated on April 12, 1989, by Rajan Mehta—a mechanical engineering graduate from IIT Bombay—with a mission to manufacture precision automotive components that could meet the stringent demands of India's emerging passenger vehicle industry.

The company's first customer was Tata Motors, supplying machined engine brackets and cover plates for the Tata 407 commercial vehicle. Within five years, the client roster expanded to include Bajaj Auto, Mahindra & Mahindra, and Maruti Suzuki.

The 1990s were a transformative decade. AutoVerse invested heavily in CNC machining technology from Mazak and DMG Mori, allowing it to compete on quality with global Tier-1 suppliers. The company achieved ISO 9001 certification in 2001—one of the earliest automotive component manufacturers in Maharashtra to do so.

The 2000s brought international expansion. AutoVerse established supply relationships with Volkswagen Group and Ford India, requiring compliance with VDA 6.3 process audits and PPAP documentation standards. A second plant was commissioned in Chennai in 2007 to serve the rapidly growing South Indian automotive corridor.

By 2015, AutoVerse had achieved IATF 16949:2016 certification and was exporting components to tier-2 suppliers in Germany, the United Kingdom, and Japan. The company's third facility—a dedicated casting and finishing plant in Manesar, Haryana—opened in 2019 to serve the NCR automotive cluster.

Today, under the leadership of Arjun Mehta (Managing Director) and a board of professional directors, AutoVerse continues its journey toward Industry 4.0—implementing IoT-connected machine monitoring, real-time SPC, and AI-based vision inspection across all three plants.

Vision & Mission

+

Our Vision: To be the most trusted precision manufacturing partner for global automotive OEMs—recognized for our engineering excellence, quality commitment, and sustainable manufacturing practices.

Our Mission: To manufacture world-class automotive components that enable our customers to build safer, more efficient, and more reliable vehicles—while fostering a culture of innovation, continuous improvement, and employee well-being.

Our Values:

  • Precision — Every micron matters. We never compromise on dimensional accuracy or surface quality.
  • Integrity — We are transparent with our customers, employees, and partners in all that we do.
  • Innovation — We actively invest in new processes, technologies, and materials to stay ahead.
  • Sustainability — We measure our success not only in profit but in our environmental and social impact.
  • People First — Our 2,000+ employees are our greatest asset. We invest in their growth and safety every day.
  • Customer Focus — We exist to help our customers succeed. Their success drives every decision we make.

Our Strengths

+

AutoVerse's competitive edge is built on five core strengths that our customers consistently cite as the reasons they remain with us through product generations and market cycles:

  • Advanced 5-axis CNC machining with ±0.005mm capability
  • In-house tooling design & manufacture for faster lead times
  • 140+ Mazak, DMG Mori, and Okuma machining centers
  • Real-time SPC with automated out-of-tolerance alerts
  • Zeiss CMM and optical inspection on 100% of critical features
  • IATF 16949, ISO 9001, ISO 14001, and OHSAS 18001 certified
  • 3 strategically located plants across India
  • JIT delivery with 98.7% on-time record over 5 years
  • 120-person dedicated R&D and application engineering team
  • Customer portal for real-time order tracking and quality records
  • Renewable energy powering 40% of total facility consumption
  • Zero liquid discharge at all three manufacturing sites
